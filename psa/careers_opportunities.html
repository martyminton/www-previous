Title: Cultural/Language Instructors

Location: Camp Lejeune , NC and Camp Pendleton, CA

Job Description:
ProSol Associates, LLC is looking for qualified candidates to fill the following positions in NC and CA.

Experience in the Iraqi culture and/or language to train troops preparing to deploy. Ideal candidate will be a former Marine, or have indepth understanding of the Marine Corps culture; a former staff NCO, company grade officer, and have operational/ground experience interacting with Iraqis in country. Secondary to the Iraqi language and culture instructors are Afghan language and culture instructors. Language instructors should be proficient in Iraqi dialects, and also fluent in English. These positions are mainly located at Camp Lejeune, NC and Camp Pendleton, CA. Linguists are encouraged to apply. All candidates must be able to take instruction, and absolutely must be a team player with a clear understanding of Marine Corps terminology, philosophy, and mission.

Clearance: None Required

Title: Recruiter

Location: Alexandria, VA

Job Description:
Coordinates the professional and managerial staffing activity of the company, including internal and external recruitment and placement, college recruiting, and armed forces officer outplacement recruitment. Coordinates with Manager—Equal Employment Programs so that recruitment programs are consistent with the corporation's commitment as an equal opportunity employer.

Essential Functions:

  • Directs the internal and external exempt recruitment and placement activities of the company, including university and college recruiting, masters' level recruitment and at outplacement centers for armed forces officers.
  • Develops recruitment and placement policies, systems and procedures including strategies and selection techniques. Reviews and approves all exempt employment advertisements. Negotiates employment fee structures with major placement agencies.
  • Assists in implementation of affirmative action plans and special recruitment goals and programs as they relate to the hiring of women, minorities, veterans, or individuals with disabilities.
  • Acts as liaison in representing the company in dealings with federal and state agencies supporting employment goals, and with special interest groups.
  • Organizes and conducts training programs in effective employment interviewing consistent with current laws and regulations and provisions of the Americans with Disabilities Act.
  • Recruits and screens candidates for exempt positions at the corporate office.

Additional Responsibilities:

  • May act as an interviewing resource person for candidates for division positions.
  • May substitute for the manager—EEO compliance in his or her absence.

Qualifications:

  • Bachelor's degree, or equivalent, in human resources management or counseling. Extensive interviewing experience (at least three years) for exempt candidates for positions for within government contracting, military or IT field.
  • Excellent interviewing skills. Must have a thorough knowledge of recruitment and selection techniques and requirements of the federal Selection Guidelines and the Americans with Disabilities Act.

Education:

Bachelor's Degree from a four-year college or university with coursework in sales, marketing, business administration or related field. Advanced degree in any discipline acceptable as well. 10+ years government sales.

Clearance: Prior military experience and a Top Secret Clearance are a plus

All interested applicants should send an updated resume in Word along with salary range, availability date to info@prosolassociates.com.

Title: Secure Flight Analyst - Intel Analyst

Location: Annapolis Junction, MD and Colorado Springs, CO

Job Description:
ProSol Associates is looking for qualified candidates to fill the following positions supporting the Department of Homeland Security in Annapolis Junction, MD and Colorado Spring, CO.

The Secure Flight Analyst will provide analytical, indications & warning (I&W), and operational intelligence in support to the TSA Office of Transportation Assessment and Credentialing (TTAC) for the Secure Flight Program. The Intelligence Analyst must be able to access intelligence resources and will interface on a regular basis with representatives from the TSA office. Must perform advanced threat assessments and conduct timely research. Must be skilled in developing, analyzing, evaluating Counterterrorism-related programs, policies, and procedures at the national level. Must hold a current, active and in-scope TS/SCI security clearance. Must be able to work shift-work and weekend assignments.

Qualifications: Must have 3-6 years of experience working in the intelligence community with a combination of hands-on experience to include; education in Counterterrorism, I&W, and terrorism threat analysis Desire experience in utilizing and analyzing all-source intelligence. Requires excellent leadership and ability to perform under pressure and to meet short deadlines. Excellent writing and oral communication skills and extensive experience with Microsoft Office application. Duty hours will require shift-work and weekend assignments. Position will participate in shift work.

Clearance: Top Secret based on SSBI (SCI preferred)
