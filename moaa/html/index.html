Letter from the

President

Throughout the year, we publish numerous materials meant to educate both our members and our lawmakers on the importance of military rights and how the Military Officers Association of America (MOAA) is protecting them. But the one publication that stands out to me is MOAA's Annual Letter.

This personal publication affords MOAA the rare opportunity to not only reach out to our members nationwide but to also reflect and anticipate — to look back on our victories in Washington and to look ahead at those that are to come.

Below, you'll find a summary of what we've achieved together in the past year, from how many messages we've sent to Congress to how many military children we've put through college. You'll also learn how you can get involved with MOAA and gain the most from your relationship with us. Whether it means joining a local chapter, participating as a board member, or sharing a message on our Facebook page, it's all about connecting.

  As you read through this annual letter, my greatest hope is for you to take pride in the organization that takes so much pride in you.

Sincerely,

Advocacy

Winning Battles

MOAA's greatest mission is to fight for the men and women who protect this country. And most of that fighting takes place squarely in Washington, D.C., where we protect your interests and amplify your voice on Capitol Hill. Below are just a few of MOAA's legislative wins from the past year alone:

  • Won full Consumer Price Index cost-of-living adjustment (1.5%) for retirees, survivors, and disabled veterans
  • Established a new grassroots record, generating more than 1 million messages to Congress
  • Kept TRICARE affordable by defeating proposals to increase fees, deductibles, and pharmacy copays
  • Avoided a 24% cut in Medicare and TRICARE payments to providers
  • Won 45 states' participation in the Interstate Compact on Educational Opportunity for Military Children
  • Created financial penalties for fraudulent use of unauthorized military awards and decorations under the Stolen Valor Act
  • Won $30 million in supplemental aid to school districts serving large numbers of military children

Educational Assistance

Education: A Top Priority

MOAA is committed to serving the families of those who serve this nation, and one of the many ways we do that is through educational assistance for military children.

In 2013, MOAA distributed nearly $9.1 million in grants and interest-free loans for more than 1,700 military families. Thanks to the dedication of our members, MOAA has ensured bright futures for the families that protect our own.

Career
Transition

New Beginnings

The transition from the military to the civilian workforce is different for everyone, and now MOAA is launching even more tools and workshops to make the move that much easier, including:

  • Two career fairs, four virtual fairs, and three networking events
  • MOAA's newly introduced Military Executive Transition (MET) Workshop, which digs deeper into skills like negotiating salaries and working with recruiters
  • On-staff financial experts who can help you maneuver through investment planning
  • MOAA's Interview Practice, powered by InterviewStream© — a state-of-the-art tool designed to help you put your best face forward during the interview process
  • MOAA's Tips for Lifelong Caregiving Guide, which provides caregivers with strong direction on their legal and financial needs

Chapters

Making local connections

With more than 400 chapters, 57,000 chapter members, and 380,000 national members, MOAA is a force to be reckoned with. And the larger our numbers, the greater our impact on Capitol Hill and the stronger our local communities.

One of our latest projects, Community Blueprint, is a MOAA-sponsored national service project designed to help servicemembers, veterans, and their families across America. Employment Initiatives for
military spouses

In addition to our three spouse symposia scheduled to take place this year around the country, MOAA has teamed up with Syracuse University's Institute for Veterans and Military Families to launch a detailed Military Spouse Employment Survey, measuring military spouses' professional needs and obstacles. Now, we're taking the results of that study and spreading the word, sharing our findings with government officials who can help impact future policy change.

Voices for
America's
Troops®

Voices for America's Troops (Voices), a nonprofit subsidiary of MOAA, recognizes the demands placed on our all-volunteer force. Members of Voices are individuals who aren't eligible for MOAA membership but still share an interest in sustaining support for our servicemembers. Voices continues to grow every year, ending 2013 with 45,000 members.
