  • Use our tools and resources to navigate your retirement path.

    Throughout your life, you’ve paved your own path—finding the things that mean the most to you. The road to retirement isn’t any different. This is your time to plan ahead for what you want then. Whether you want to travel or work part-time, AARP’s tools and resources can help you plot your course and get moving toward the retirement you want.

    This 5-step guide will help you gather the information and resources you’ll need along the way.

    Get started withStep 1
  • Step 1

    Decide what retirement means for you.

    First things first, what do you want to do? Make a list of your retirement objectives and important goals. Don’t limit yourself by thinking financially right now. Focus on ideas and be as specific as you can. Perhaps include why you want to achieve these goals. This exercise will help to define what retirement means to you.

  • Step 2

    Maximize your monthly Social Security benefits.

    For many of us, Social Security is the biggest source of our retirement income, and deciding when to claim is a big decision. Working a little longer and delaying your claim will provide a much more secure retirement for life. Weigh the pros and cons of working longer, and get comfortable with your decision. The next steps will focus on filling income gaps that Social Security doesn't meet so you can realize your goals.

  • Step 3

    Calculate what you’ll need to get from A to B.

    Funding your retirement means seeing your overall financial picture. Look at how much you have in the bank, what you bring home each month, your savings rate and the size of your retirement account. Then plug those numbers, along with your estimated Social Security benefits, into AARP's free Retirement Calculator. This will help you identify your savings goal.

  • Step 4

    Close the gap between income and expenses.

    Now that you know how much income you can count on each month and how much you need to live comfortably in retirement, it's time to figure out how to plug any holes. Start by designing a budget, cutting costs, and using your savings to pay off debt and — if possible — to buy an annuity to provide guaranteed income for life. Think about what skills, hobbies and passions you could turn into a second career — an encore career — that could help you pad your income.
